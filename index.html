About Me

I am a computer science student at Northeastern University, Boston, MA, with an expected graduation date of May 2026.

Also, I'm currently a research assistant in Chats Lab advised by Weiyan Shi. Before this, I have worked as an intern researcher in UESTC.

I'll join AI4EPS in UCB as a research assistant this summer and advised by Professor Weiqiang Zhu.

My Research Interests include Multi-Modal Learning, Continual Learning, and Large Language Models.

Research Interests

Multi-Modal Learning

Inspired by how humans naturally process and integrate information from different modalities, I'm developing multi-modal systems that can similarly understand and reason across diverse inputs. My work includes multi-modal emotion recognition and earthquake prediction models that combine visual, acoustic, and seismic data streams to achieve more robust and comprehensive understanding.

Continual Learning

I'm fascinated by how humans can continuously acquire new knowledge without catastrophically forgetting previous information. My research explores continual learning approaches, including continual prompt learning and continual multi-modal learning, aiming to develop AI systems that can adaptively learn over time while maintaining performance on previously learned tasks.

Large Language Models

I investigate how large language models can be enhanced for more faithful, interpretable, and personalized interactions. Through projects like the LLM Persuasion Arena, I explore how these models can be fine-tuned to understand complex human intentions while maintaining safety guardrails, pushing the boundaries of what's possible in human-AI collaboration.

Research Philosophy

I believe in building efficient, high-performing models that can be deployed in resource-constrained environments while maintaining accuracy and reliability.
