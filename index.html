About Me

I am a computer science student at Northeastern University, Boston, MA, with an expected graduation date of May 2026.

I've been involved in several research projects supervised by professors from Northeastern University, UESTC, Carnegie Mellon University, Imperial College London, and the University of Cambridge.

My major courses include Machine Learning and Data Mining, Artificial Intelligence, Object-Oriented Design, Algorithms and Data, Database Design, and Probability and Statistics.

Research Interests

Large Language Models

Working on LLM Persuasion Arena, a platform where users can test different persuasion techniques in a simulated conversation with various LLM agents. Implemented personalization modules and evaluation matrices for testing LLM capabilities.

Multi-Modal Learning

Developing multi-modal models for earthquake prediction by integrating diverse seismic data sources into unified AI-driven systems. Also worked on multi-modal emotion recognition achieving 78.3% accuracy by combining audio and video features.

Computer Vision

Conducted research on landmark recognition/retrieval and wild animal classification (92.2% accuracy), exploring various feature enhancement methods including CBAM, DANet, and BAM to improve performance.

Research Philosophy

I believe in building efficient, high-performing models that can be deployed in resource-constrained environments while maintaining accuracy and reliability.
